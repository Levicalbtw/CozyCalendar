\f0\fs24 \cf0 # Product Requirements Document: Cozy Calendar MVP\
\
## Product Overview\
\
**App Name:** Cozy Calendar\
**Tagline:** Organize your life exactly how you want it, making productivity feel like a creative release instead of a chore.\
**Launch Goal:** Successfully finish and launch the project, get some initial signups, and gather positive feedback.\
**Target Launch:** 2 weeks from today\
\
## Who It's For\
\
### Primary User: The Creative Organizer\
Youth and young adults who are somewhat tech-savvy but prioritize aesthetics and ease of use over complex technical features. \
\
**Their Current Pain:**\
- Existing productivity apps feel rigid, boring, and tedious.\
- They lack a creative outlet in their daily organization.\
- They struggle to find a single app that adds structure to their life *their* way.\
\
**What They Need:**\
- A highly intuitive, user-friendly interface.\
- Extreme customization to make the space feel deeply personal.\
- A seamless blend of scheduling (calendar) and reflecting (journal).\
\
### Example User Story\
"Meet Alex, a creative student who struggles with finding a tool that really adds structure to their life because everything out there is just a boring grid. Every day they feel unmotivated to plan. They need a customizable, scrapbook-like workspace so they can structure their life exactly how they need it and actually enjoy the process."\
\
## The Problem We're Solving\
\
People, especially younger demographics and creatives, want to be organized, but traditional tools drain their energy. When an app forces you into a rigid structure, organizing feels like homework. We are solving the "boredom of productivity" by turning daily planning into a creative, cozy, and highly personalized experience.\
\
**Why Existing Solutions Fall Short:**\
- **TimeTree / Fantastical**: Highly functional but aesthetically sterile; they lack the tactile, creative scrapbook feel and journal integration.\
- **Paper Planners**: Great for creativity, but lack digital convenience, reminders, and easy editing.\
\
## User Journey\
\
### Discovery \uc0\u8594  First Use \u8594  Success\
\
1. **Discovery Phase**\
   - How they find us: Social media (TikTok/Pinterest/Instagram) showing off aesthetic digital journal spreads.\
   - What catches their attention: The cozy, scrapbook vibe and page-flipping animations.\
   - Decision trigger: It's completely free to use.\
\
2. **Onboarding (First 5 Minutes)**\
   - Land on: A warm, inviting homepage.\
   - First action: Picking their initial aesthetic/color scheme and adding a sticker to today's date.\
   - Quick win: Seeing their personalized digital journal page come to life immediately.\
\
3. **Core Usage Loop**\
   - Trigger: Needing to check their schedule or wanting to journal at the end of the day.\
   - Action: Customizing the day's page with text, stickers, and photos.\
   - Reward: A beautiful, visually pleasing record of their schedule and thoughts.\
   - Investment: The more days they customize, the more it feels like *their* personal diary.\
\
4. **Success Moment**\
   - "Aha!" moment: When they flip back through past days and see a beautiful, cohesive scrapbook of their life.\
   - Share trigger: Wanting to show off their aesthetic layout on social media.\
\
## MVP Features\
\
### Must Have for Launch\
\
#### 1. Integrated Calendar & Journal Interface\
- **What:** A central view where users can see their calendar events and write journal entries on the same page.\
- **User Story:** As a user, I want to see my schedule and journal in one place so that I don't have to switch between boring, separate apps.\
- **Success Criteria:**\
  - [ ] Users can click on a date and see both a scheduling area and a free-text journaling area.\
  - [ ] Includes analog page transitions (flipping/ripping).\
- **Priority:** P0 (Critical)\
\
#### 2. Extreme Visual Customization (Scrapbook Engine)\
- **What:** The ability to change colors, fonts, backgrounds, and freely place digital stickers/photos.\
- **User Story:** As a user, I want to customize my page with stickers and colors so that it matches my current vibe and feels cozy.\
- **Success Criteria:**\
  - [ ] Users can change the background color/pattern.\
  - [ ] Users can drag and drop stickers/images onto the page.\
- **Priority:** P0 (Critical)\
\
#### 3. Ultimate User-Friendliness\
- **What:** A frictionless, intuitive UI that requires zero tutorials to understand.\
- **User Story:** As a non-technical user, I want the app to be instantly obvious how to use so that I don't get frustrated and quit.\
- **Success Criteria:**\
  - [ ] Anyone can figure out how to add an entry and a sticker within 10 seconds of landing on the page.\
- **Priority:** P0 (Critical)\
\
### NOT in MVP (Saving for Later)\
- **Undecided Future Features**: We are keeping the blinders on for the 2-week sprint. Any complex ideas (like social sharing, mobile app ports, complex API integrations) that come up during development will be pushed to Version 2.\
\
*Why we're waiting: Keeps MVP focused and launchable in 14 days.*\
\

| Signups / Users | 10-50 users | Free database tracking |\
| Qualitative Feedback | Positive sentiment | Direct messages, comments, or a simple feedback form |\
\
## Look & Feel\
\
**Design Vibe:** Scrapbook-like, creative, cozy.\
\
**Visual Principles:**\
1. **Warmth:** Soft colors, cozy aesthetics, avoiding harsh tech-startup blues/grays.\
2. **Tactile:** Elements should feel like physical objects (paper textures, sticker drop shadows, page flips).\
3. **Unstructured Structure:** The underlying grid exists for the calendar, but users can break it visually with their customizations.\
\
**Key Screens/Pages:**\
1. **Landing/Home View**: The main calendar view (monthly/weekly).\
2. **Daily Canvas**: The zoomed-in daily view where the heavy journaling and scrapbooking happens.\
